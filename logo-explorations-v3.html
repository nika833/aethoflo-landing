Logo Explorations · 03 — Isobar, pushed

Six ways to groove.

Asymmetrical, rounded, thick-and-thin takes on the Isobar direction — each one trying to capture the idea that training doesn't arrive on a metronome; it arrives on your rhythm.

Why asymmetry is on-thesis. The aethoflo promise is that learning lands at your rhythm, not a symmetric schedule. A mark built on five even bars would say the opposite: cadence, equality, calendar. These marks swell and contract the way a real journey does — heavier at the first plan review, lighter at a Wednesday check-in, warmer in the middle where most of the work lives. Thick/thin isn't decoration. It's the product.
01 Swell breath

Five bars, but each has its own weight: hairline at the edges, thick in the middle where the work is real. The bars aren't centered — they align to the left, like a paragraph of type. Stable horizon + groovy pulse at the heart.

02 Tide offset rhythm

Four pill-shaped bars, staggered left-to-right. Each one starts further in, ends further out — like a wave rolling toward shore. The third bar is the accent, slightly fatter than the rest. Groovy, off-beat, deeply rounded.

Three thick pills stacked, with a thin orange pill slipped between them — a moment of clarity punctuating dense material. Reads as a learning schedule: three big weeks, one sharp insight that cuts through. Typographic feel; sits beautifully next to a serif.

05 Pluck disturbed

Three rounded bars — but the middle one is bowed like a plucked string right after contact. The disturbance IS the moment. The other bars are still, the accent one is living. Most narrative of the set: a literal "you touched something, and it responded."

06 Terrain uneven

Bars of wildly varying lengths and weights — like a city skyline or a week of meetings charted. The middle is thickest and longest (the core accent); the outer bars are hairline. Most expressive, most asymmetric; implies the messy reality of real work.
