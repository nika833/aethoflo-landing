The Problem

The right training at the wrong time
is the same as no training at all.

Providers carry an enormous amount — clinical skills, client relationships, compliance, and ongoing development — all at once. When training is front-loaded before they have the context to apply it, it doesn't stick. The challenge isn't intent. It's the complexity of orchestrating all of it well.

01 · FRONT-LOADED

Front-loaded onboarding

Too much training is delivered upfront — before providers have the context to absorb or apply it. By the time it's relevant, it's already been forgotten.

02 · DISCONNECTED

Out of sync with the journey

Modules are assigned by role and delivered on a fixed schedule — not calibrated to where a provider actually is in their development.

03 · INVISIBLE

Tied to a schedule, not a journey

Resources aren't connected to where providers are in their development — creating a gap between what's trained and what actually improves quality of service delivery.
