Logo Explorations · 07 — unified flow glyph

One shape, two states.

Six directions built from a single silhouette — the hollow outline and the orange fill are the same body, not three separate droplets. Where they overlap, the mark earns its meaning.

What changes. Instead of stacking discrete shapes, the icon becomes one continuous glyph with two densities. The outline is the vessel; the orange is the flow inside it. That's the aethoflo story in a single form: a container for learning and the moment filling it up. Same silhouette at every scale — no arrangement to decipher, just one shape to recognize.
F1Pearfill level

A single gourd-like silhouette in outline — with orange filling the lower portion like liquid in a vessel. Narrow at the top, broad at the base. Reads as "the container is receiving the moment."
