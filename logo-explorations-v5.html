Logo Explorations · 05 — Techified lava system

One silhouette, many weights.

If Ascent and Glow are the lava vibe, the way to make them feel professional is to stop thinking of them as a single icon and start thinking of them as a family — the way Dropbox, Vercel, and Linear each built a system around one shape. Solid, outline, half-filled, contour, echoed — each treatment earns a different role in the brand.

How a graphic designer would build this out. Pick one canonical silhouette. Then commission three or four treatments: the hero mark (color, gradient, full-bleed), the stroke mark (single-weight outline for cards, print, embroidery), an "inside" variant (contour lines, grid, echo rings — the techy one), and a reductive favicon mark. The system reads as disciplined even when you use different weights in different places — the silhouette keeps them all related.
Ascent family three rising droplets · 3 treatments

Full-color gradient plate, cream droplets. The brand's front-door icon — app stores, OG images, merch. Warm and recognizable at a glance.
