Logo Explorations · 08 — asymmetric overlap

Side by side, meeting in the middle.

Two shapes of different sizes. Where they overlap, the orange lives — and the black outline disappears through that zone so the orange reads as shared, not bordered. The overlap is the product.

Why this works. Two separate forms admit what the product actually does: brings together things that weren't together before — a learner and the right moment, a plan and the real work. The orange lens is the sweet spot where they meet. No outline bisects it, so the eye reads the overlap as belonging to both shapes equally. Asymmetry keeps it from feeling like a corporate venn diagram — the smaller circle is the moment; the larger is the provider receiving it.
